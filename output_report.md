## Final Report

For supply-side constraints, the evidence shows that OPEC+ agreed to a historic cut of nearly 10% of global oil production to counter demand loss from COVID-19 [F1]. The same production deal was described as a major effort to stabilize markets and end a price war between producers [F2]. Other extracted findings point to regional supply disruptions: Middle East Gulf oil producers deepened output cuts by over 5 million barrels per day after Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic disruption [F3], and storage pressure forced Saudi Arabia and other top producers to cut 2-2.5 million barrels per day [F4].

Additional findings also connect OPEC+ decisions to price pressure. One source reported an OPEC+ production cut of 1.66 million barrels per day [F5], with Russia's previously stated 500,000 barrel-per-day reduction included in the overall cut [F6]. However, the evidence is not one-directional: another finding says seven OPEC+ countries, including Russia and Saudi Arabia, increased their oil production quotas [F7], and that this quota increase was expected [F8]. Taken together, the verified evidence suggests that changing producer quotas and production discipline are central to oil-price movements, but the direction depends on whether current decisions restrict or expand supply.

The pipeline did not extract verified findings for demand growth, geopolitical risk, currency effects, investor sentiment, or futures-market speculation in this run. Those topics remain important parts of the original question, but this report can only make supported claims from the verified evidence above.
